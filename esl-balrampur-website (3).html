Read this short passage

Meera lived in a small village near Balrampur. Every morning, she walked two kilometres to the river to fetch water before school. She loved reading old newspapers that her neighbour gave her, even though she could not understand every word. One day, her teacher told her about free English classes starting in the village. Meera was excited but also worried — she had never studied English before. Still, she decided to attend the first class. Slowly, she began to understand simple sentences, then short conversations. Within a few months, she could introduce herself confidently in English. Her teacher said the biggest step was not the grammar — it was Meera deciding to walk into that first class.
